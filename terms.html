1. What GroundTruth is — and what it is not

GroundTruth is a peer-attestation platform. It lets Ghanaian landowners record their plot boundaries, collect endorsements from people who know the land, and surface community trust signals to others who might transact with them.

GroundTruth does not determine ownership. Trust signals on the platform — endorsements, verifier badges, acreage records, dispute history — are community indicators, not legal title. Only the courts of Ghana and the Lands Commission can adjudicate land ownership. Nothing on the platform creates, transfers, or proves legal title.

A plot with a strong trust score on GroundTruth tells you that a lot of community signal supports the listed owner's claim. It does not tell you that claim is legally registered. Buyers, lenders, and other interested parties should perform their own due diligence with the Lands Commission and qualified surveyors before entering any transaction.

7. Endorsements and disputes

Endorsements are statements you make in your own name, attesting that you know the land and recognise the named owner's claim. They carry weight on the platform based on who you are and how you are connected to the plot. Lying in an endorsement is a violation of these terms and may also have consequences under Ghanaian law (e.g. defamation, false witness).

Disputes are public claims that a plot record is wrong or contested. Filing a dispute triggers a temporary trust penalty on the plot until the dispute is resolved off-platform. Resolution happens through courts, traditional authorities, the Lands Commission, or mutual agreement — never on the platform itself. We just surface the conflict; we don't adjudicate it.
